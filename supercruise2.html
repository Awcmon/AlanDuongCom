SUPERCRUISE 2

I thought the original SUPERCRUISE felt good, but didn't have enough substance. I couldn't sell it.

OpenFrameworks also had its own problems that made it difficult for me to implement certain quality of life features (different resolutions changable on runtime, borderless windows, easy screen stretching, etc) and whether or not the executable's ability to run was a bit finnicky. I switched to Unity.

Planes should shoot at you and not just ram into you. I didnt want enemy planes to just leisurely float by, hurling bullets at the player at the speed of a snowball. Planes move fast and bullets move fast so I wanted this game to be fast.

The warning indicators allow everything to move fast while still giving the player the time to react. It tries to, anyways. That's the core of the game.

The best way to test if a game is viable and fun is to make it and play it. And so, I teamed up with a friend to help me with the art and made a prototype.

SUPERCRUISE 3

It wasn't fun.

I sent it to a number of my friends who I trusted to give me honest feedback. They confirmed what I felt in my heart-- it was the sort of game where if you're asked if it's "good or bad," you might say, "well, it isn't bad." However, if the question was, "would you want to play more?" the answer would be quick "no."

I tried to think of a way to make it more fun. I thought that the main problem is that simply dodging is boring. The player should be incentivized to kill enemies, and simply having a points system isn't enough to encourage that. I could make it so enemy planes circle back after they buzz past you, but something like that just feels awkward (it's strange that the only player is moving in a straight line when nothing else is) and is hard to convey to the player (it isn't so obvious that the enemy coming from behind you is the same one that passed you from the front 30 seconds ago).

SUPERCRUISE 2 was fundamentally flawed. There was no other solution than to cut my losses-- I would never find this game fun.

For many people, it hurts to throw away your own hard work. I've personally done that many times, so I've gotten over it long ago. However, I couldn't stand throwing away the art my friend drew for this game. I tried to visualize a game using his art that I would find fun.
